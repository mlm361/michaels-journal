<p><br />I canceled my <strong>Paramount+</strong> subscription on
Saturday, just before the renewal day yesterday, and I’m thinking of
dumping a few more. I canceled <strong>Apple TV+</strong> a few months
ago. There are TV programs I like on both, but I just can't justify it
anymore. These subscriptions are like vampires, siphoning a few dollars
here and there until my budget is bled dry. These little charges are
starting to eat away at my budget.</p>
<a name='more'></a>
<p>There have been numerous articles about how our society is turning into
one big rental/subscription economy, where we don’t own anything—we just
rent it. To keep track of my expenses, I now maintain a spreadsheet of
all my subscriptions, noting whether I pay monthly, yearly, or on a
fixed term. Whenever possible, I choose <strong>y</strong>early payments
since they usually come with a small discount.</p>

<p>I've also started investing in a couple of Raspberry Pis to self-host as
much as possible. But there are some services I won’t compromise on. One
of them is my managed Mastodon instance from
<strong>Masto.host</strong>. The service removes the strain of
maintenance, even for a single-user instance, and it’s actually cheaper
than my previous setup using the smallest available<strong>
DigitalOcean</strong> server. Choosing <strong>Masto.host</strong> was
an excellent decision—<strong>Hugo Gameiro</strong> does a fantastic job
with managed Mastodon hosting.</p>

<p>Another service <strong>I wouldn’t think of cutting</strong> is
<strong>Micro.blog</strong>. I only recently started using it, but I’m
already very impressed. <strong>Manton Reece</strong>, like Hugo, runs a
tight and reliable service. I don’t mind paying for superior service and
time-saving convenience.</p>

<p>That said, there are plenty of subscriptions—<em>or as I call them,
leases</em>—that I don’t mind cutting. I hate car leases and have never
had one; I’d rather<em> own my stuff than rent it</em>. My parents,
brother, and sister-in-law all tried leasing a car once, and they
learned their lesson, too.</p>
