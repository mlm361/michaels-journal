<p>I really need a new chair. I bought a <strong>Secretlab Titan</strong> chair in 2020 at the height of the supply shortages and COVID-19. I had to wait a few months for it and paid almost <strong>$500</strong>. I figured that since I work remotely a couple of days a week and would be sitting in it when I’m home from work, it would be a worthwhile investment.</p>
<p>I should have done more research.</p>
<p>For the first year, the chair was alright—the seat was a little uncomfortable, but the backrest and headrest were fine. Fast forward three years, and one day, while sitting in the chair, I suddenly found myself on the floor. One of the base spokes/feet broke and sent me tumbling.</p>
<p>To their credit, Secretlab sent me replacement parts, but ever since then, the chair has been going downhill. The hydraulic cylinder is failing—if the chair is facing a certain way, it sinks all the way down. The armrests are also wearing out and becoming rough.</p>
<p>I say all this to emphasize that I want and need a new chair. But with the current state of the U.S. economy, I’m hesitant to make a purchase right now.</p>
<p>Plus, what should I get? I’ve been leaning toward Herman Miller or Steelcase since I want something comfortable and long-lasting.</p>
<p>Does anyone have any suggestions?</p>
