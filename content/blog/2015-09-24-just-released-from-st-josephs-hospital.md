<p>Hello All, </p> <p>I was in the hospital for 3 days this week. I had my first ambulance ride to the hospital. I suffered A-Fib. The first time, I really felt bad where I was worried. They were able to stop the A-Fib and put me on medication. At least it WAS NOT a heart attack or a stoke. Thanks to my coworker, boss and my family for everything. Thanks to Jesus and God for saying it was not time for me to go. </p> <p>Apparently this is common and serious. My weight had nothing to do with it. My hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) may have contributed to the A-Fib, but it certainly did not help. </p> <p>I am currently on an antiarrhythmic and a blood thinner. St. Joseph’s staff was great, Tampa Fire Rescue was great and work treated me great.
